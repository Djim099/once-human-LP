Getting Started

Your First Hour

Once Human doesn't bury you in tutorials. Here's what to focus on when you first land in the contaminated world.

  1. 1

    Land and Breathe

    Your starting zone is manageable. Collect basic resources — wood, stone, fiber. Ignore the urge to explore immediately. You need a workbench first.

  2. 2

    Claim Your Territory

    Use your Territory Marker early. Find slightly elevated ground near water. This will become your base — choose deliberately rather than conveniently.

  3. 3

    Build the Core Four

    Workbench, Furnace, Storage, Shelter. These four structures are your minimum viable base. Everything else builds on top of them.

  4. 4

    Find Your First Deviant

    Deviants are your companions. Capture your first one early — even a low-tier Deviant can make early exploration significantly safer and more efficient.

  5. 5

    Enter the First Contaminated Zone

    Once you have basic armor and a ranged weapon, push into the nearest marked contaminated zone. What you find inside will answer your first real questions about this world.

Everything You Want to Know

Yes. Once Human is free to download and play. There are cosmetic options available, but none of the core gameplay, progression, or content is locked behind a paywall. You can experience everything the game has to offer without spending a cent.
Solo play is fully supported and deliberately designed to be satisfying. The world scales appropriately, and many players prefer the focused, high-stakes intensity of solo survival. Squad play adds a different dimension — especially for world bosses and deep contaminated zones — but it's never required.
No. PvP in Once Human is opt-in. The default PvE experience keeps you focused on the world itself — contaminated zones, aberrations, and environmental threats. Territory Wars and PvP modes exist for those who want them, but they're never imposed on players who prefer pure survival.
Deviants are anomalous entities you can capture and bond with. They serve as companions, base workers, and ability augmenters. Each Deviant has unique skills — some enhance crafting, some defend your base, others augment your combat abilities in the field. Building a strong Deviant roster is one of the most rewarding meta-progressions in the game.
You claim territory using a Territory Marker, then build freely within that zone. Construction is modular — walls, floors, roofs, furniture, power infrastructure, turrets, traps. As you progress, you unlock more complex blueprints. Your base can be a minimalist survival outpost or an elaborate fortress, depending on your playstyle and ambitions.
Both. There's a genuine narrative with missions, lore, encrypted terminals, and a central mystery about the origin of Stardust and what happened to humanity. The story is layered and rewards players who actively engage with it. But the sandbox survives on its own merits if you prefer to ignore the narrative and simply build and explore.
Once Human is optimized to run on a wide range of hardware. Minimum specs: Intel Core i5-4460 / AMD FX-6300, 8GB RAM, GTX 970 / RX 470, 50GB storage. Recommended specs: Intel Core i7-6700K / Ryzen 5 1600, 16GB RAM, GTX 1080 / RX 5700. The game also supports mobile platforms.
Yes. The development team actively releases seasonal content updates, new zones, new Deviants, balance patches, and community-driven improvements. The game has a development roadmap that's publicly shared with the player community, and major updates typically arrive every 6-8 weeks.
In PvE mode, your base is protected from other players. Stardust anomalies can damage structures, but the progression systems are designed so that setbacks are recoverable.
